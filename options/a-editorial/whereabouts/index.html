§ I  How it plays

Three minutes
From the first spin to the last reveal — small enough to be a habit.

A new puzzle each day, drawn on the same warm globe.

Each morning, Whereabouts hands you five small geographies. The 10K equirectangular map is wrapped on a real sphere — drag to spin, pinch to zoom, tap to drop a pin. You'll have a place to find: a country, a capital, a city, a landmark. Get close and you keep your streak; miss by an ocean and you'll learn something. The whole round takes about as long as a coffee.

There are no accounts and nothing to sign. Your streak, your XP, your earned badges — everything lives on the device, in UserDefaults. You can play the daily for free, forever. Pro just opens the back catalog and the themed map packs.

§ III  The globe itself

A real sphere, not a square pretending.

The 10K equirectangular map is wrapped on an actual 3D globe in RealityKit. You'll feel it: the parallax when you spin Antarctica past the rim, the way the lighting catches the Pacific. It's a small thing and the whole point.

  • Drag to spin · the world keeps inertia
  • Pinch to zoom · down to street-aware
  • Tap to drop a pin · long-press to nudge

§ IV  The morning ritual

Open it with the coffee. Done by the second sip.

Whereabouts is built around a daily — five rounds, about three minutes. A streak for showing up, XP for getting close, badges for the milestones that aren't announced until you find them.

  • Streaks · A reason to come back without nagging.
  • XP & Badges · 17 to find. We won't tell you what they are.
  • Leaderboard · Weekly, all-time, friends — opt-in only.

§ V  The reveal

Wrong is interesting too.

Each round ends with a measured arc from your pin to the answer — distance in kilometers, points earned, and a single sentence about the place that you'll remember when it shows up at trivia night. We try to write them well.

  • Distance · Great-circle, in kilometers.
  • Score · Closer is more, with a soft floor for the wild guesses.
  • Footnote · A sentence about the place — written by us.
